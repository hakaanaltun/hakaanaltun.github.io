The mother was at the corner, or would be by first light, and would stand there to the end whether or not there was a shoulder beside hers. To arrive after, to stand at a heap when the standing that mattered was already done—there was no sense to be made of it. So the going stopped here.

The way back was the way out in reverse and shorter for being known: the shore road the other direction, the last ferry, the train from the corner, the five minutes’ walk, the key back across the landing.

It was an evening like the evenings when there had been no going—the flat, the kettle, the record where it had been left. From the outside nothing marked it.

The 4:15 stayed on the board and left with the seat empty.

At first light the phone lay on the table, face up. No call was made, and none came.

Somewhere on the far side the machines started, and did not carry this far.
