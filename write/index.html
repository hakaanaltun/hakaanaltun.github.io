About this page

A blank page set in Garamond, for morning pages, drafts, letters, or whatever needs an empty sheet. A word count sits in one corner and autosave in the other: the page keeps your text in this browser as you write, and it is waiting when you come back. When a draft is done, the download button hands it to you as a plain text file.

Questions

Where does my text go?

It stays in this browser, saved to local storage on your device, and reappears when you return. Clearing the browser’s site data clears the page too, so download anything you want to keep.

Does the page autosave?

Yes—a moment after you pause typing, and again when you leave. Ctrl-S (Cmd-S on a Mac) saves on the spot, and a quiet note in the corner confirms it.

Can I save my writing as a file?

The download button in the top right saves the page as a plain .txt file named after today’s date.

Can I write in fullscreen?

Yes. The fullscreen button clears away everything but the paper, with the word count for company; moving the mouse brings the controls back.

More instruments: a word counter, a fullscreen clock with pomodoro, a breathing exercise, noise & nature sounds, the phase of the moon and İstanbul’s twilight—or see all the tools.
