The Page

A blank page for distraction-free writing.

About this page

A blank page set in Garamond, for morning pages, drafts, letters, or whatever needs an empty sheet. A word count sits in one corner and autosave in the other: the page keeps your text as you write, and it is waiting when you come back. When a draft is done, the download button hands it to you as a plain text file.

Questions

Where does my text go?

It stays in this browser, saved to local storage on your device, and reappears when you return. Clearing the browser’s site data clears the page too, so download anything you want to keep.

Does the page autosave?

Yes—a moment after you pause typing, and again when you leave. Ctrl-S (Cmd-S on a Mac) saves on the spot, and a note in the corner confirms it.

Can I save my writing as a file?

The download button in the top right saves the page as a plain .txt file named after today’s date.

Can I write in fullscreen?

Yes. The fullscreen button clears away everything but the paper, with the word count for company; moving the mouse brings the controls back.

What is on the shelf?

Six small things, borrowed from the other instruments and kept behind the lines button in the corner: a 25-and-5 writing timer whose only trace is a thin line along the top of the screen, rain and brown noise to write inside, the typographic marks, a tidy button that curls quotes and sets dashes right, a fuller count, and the light—light, dark, or following the sky over your city. Close the panel and the page is as bare as it ever was.

Does the shelf send anything anywhere?

No. The timer keeps time inside the page and never asks to send notifications, the rain and the noise are synthesized live in your browser—no recordings, nothing streamed—and the sky is computed from the clock and your time zone. Like the page itself, the shelf never lets your writing leave your device.
